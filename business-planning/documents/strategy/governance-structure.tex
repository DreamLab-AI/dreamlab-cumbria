% Executive Summary
\chapter*{Executive Summary}
\addcontentsline{toc}{chapter}{Executive Summary}

This document establishes the governance framework and organisational structure for DreamLab, ensuring effective leadership, clear accountability, and sustainable growth as we scale from startup to market leader.

\section*{Key Governance Principles}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Transparency}: Open communication and clear decision-making processes
    \item \textbf{Accountability}: Defined roles, responsibilities, and performance metrics
    \item \textbf{Innovation}: Governance that enables rather than constrains creativity
    \item \textbf{Sustainability}: Long-term thinking balanced with agile execution
    \item \textbf{Stakeholder Value}: Balancing interests of clients, team, investors, and community
\end{enumerate}

\section*{Organisational Design}

Our structure evolves through four stages:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Startup Phase (Year 1)}: Flat, agile structure with direct collaboration
    \item \textbf{Growth Phase (Year 2)}: Introduction of middle management and specialisation
    \item \textbf{Scale Phase (Year 3)}: Departmental structure with clear hierarchies
    \item \textbf{Maturity Phase (Years 4-5)}: Full corporate structure with regional divisions
\end{itemize}

\section*{Governance Bodies}

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{Board of Directors}: Strategic oversight and fiduciary responsibility
    \item \textbf{Advisory Board}: Industry expertise and strategic guidance
    \item \textbf{Executive Committee}: Operational leadership and decision-making
    \item \textbf{Innovation Council}: R\&D direction and technology strategy
    \item \textbf{Risk Committee}: Risk management and compliance oversight
\end{itemize}

This framework ensures DreamLab maintains startup agility while building the governance foundations necessary for sustainable growth and stakeholder confidence.

\section{Decision-Making Principles}

\begin{enumerate}
    \item \textbf{Empowerment}: Push decisions to the lowest appropriate level
    \item \textbf{Speed}: Favour rapid decisions with iteration over slow perfection
    \item \textbf{Data-Driven}: Base decisions on evidence where possible
    \item \textbf{Transparency}: Document and communicate decision rationale
    \item \textbf{Accountability}: Clear ownership for decisions and outcomes
\end{enumerate}

\section{Decision-Making Process}

\subsection{RAPID Framework}

We use the RAPID decision-making framework:

\begin{itemize}
    \item \textbf{R}ecommend: Who proposes the decision
    \item \textbf{A}gree: Who must agree for decision to proceed
    \item \textbf{P}erform: Who executes the decision
    \item \textbf{I}nput: Who provides input
    \item \textbf{D}ecide: Who makes the final decision
\end{itemize}
